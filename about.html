About The Organization

The story of Mutunga Foundation started in the year 2000 with Mama Silla, fondly known as Susu (Susu is Kamba for grandmother) and a drought ravaged Makueni area. On her way from Nairobi, Susu was one day stopped by six children who were hungry and she was moved by their plight. She took the children home and fed them. This day marked the first of many days when hungry children would throng her home for food. Before long, Susu was feeding up to 30 children a day! Majority of the children that came for food were either HIV/AIDS orphans or children from extremely poor families. Most were living with grandmothers and mothers who were not engaged in any income generating activity, therefore, they could not provide for their children. The circumstances of these families led Susu to seek the support of her family; she set up a small feeding center in her home. Susu then noticed these children were not in school and so she started alongside the feeding program basic literacy classes. Susu enlisted the services of a local lady to serve as a pre-school teacher. The teacher for her service was supported by Susu to enroll and complete teacher training. This feeding center became known as Mama Silla Children’s Center. Itembukatu Ndiwa Self Help Group comprising the mothers of the children was formed; they would contribute to the feeding by collecting firewood and preparing the food. In 2004, Mama Silla Children’s Center was registered as a Community Based Organization (CBO). In 2009, Mama Silla Children’s Center was registered with the NGO Board as Mutunga Foundation.

Our Team

JANET NTHAMBA SILLA

FINANCIAL DIRECTOR

At the age of 71, Janet went back to study a degree in Sociology due to her passion for public work. She graduated and immediately enrolled for a Master’s degree in Sociology. In her younger years, as was the norm, Janet had abandoned her education to get married. Her passion is to encourage school drop-outs to return to school. Due to her academic achievements at her advanced age, as well as the impact on the community, MF will use Janet as the Poster Girl for campaigns on the empowering nature of education.

TONY MBITHI

PROGRAMMS MANAGER

Tony Mbithi is a programs manager with Mutunga Foundation. His expertise in monitoring and evaluation and handling administrative aspects of programmes, is beneficial to MF. Tony’s roles are planning for activities and proactively monitoring their implementation; and reporting progress of activities at regular intervals to the Executive Director and the Board.

MARGARET MUENI OWINO

SECRETARY GENERAL

Margaret is the Secretary General of Mutunga Foundation. Her principal role is to support the Executive Director to ensure smooth running of operations. She acts as an information and reference point for the Executive Director and other Board members; ensuring conformity to legal requirements and retrieving relevant documentation.

Richard Mutuku Robert

IT Administrator

Richard Mutuku Robert is a volunteer with Mutunga Foundation. He is specialty in Information Technology.

JOSEPHINE KATHINI NDIRANGU

EXECUTIVE DIRECTOR

Josephine is the Executive Director of Mutunga Foundation. She has a passion for improving lives at the grassroots level. Josephine has a strong board ethic having served as Director in various boards. She is currently the Deputy Chairperson of the Board of Management at Masongaleni Secondary School, in Kibwezi East, Makueni County. She understands corporate governance and the virtue it brings to organizations. She believes in the principle: “No entity, economic or otherwise, can do much without the support of its people; but the very people must look to themselves first.” This motivates her to redefine the aspirations of grassroots youth and women. She is an Alumnus of United States International University-Africa, where she graduated with a Bachelor’s degree in Psychology. She is currently enrolled for a Master’s degree in International Development at Andrews University, Michigan, USA.
